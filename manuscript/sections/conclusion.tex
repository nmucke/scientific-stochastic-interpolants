We presented a model-agnostic posterior sampling method for data assimilation with flow-based generative models. Conditioning the affine Gaussian probability path on an observation tilts its target to the posterior while preserving the path structure, so the conditional score and velocity correct the prior ones by a single likelihood-score term; the marginal-preserving SDE family then yields three posterior samplers -- SI-SDE, DM-SDE, and a deterministic guided ODE -- sharing one guidance weight $\gweight_\tau=\vscoef_\tau+\tfrac12\gdiff_\tau^2$ and differing only in the diffusion. A tractable interpolated-observation likelihood supplies the score in closed form, with a covariance inflated ($\Pi$GDM-style) by the model's source covariance -- the inflation that drives accuracy on sparse observations.

\paragraph{Limitations.}
The exactness guarantees assume a linear observation operator with Gaussian noise (nonlinear operators need linearisation or sampling), Gaussian likelihood surrogates whose moments are exact but whose shape is not (except linear--Gaussian), and one-step autoregressive assimilation that omits the importance weights and future-observation conditioning of Section~\ref{sec:preliminaries}. The deterministic guided ODE is available only for flow matching. All guarantees assume a perfectly trained drift or velocity; quantifying model error on the posterior is left to future work.
