We presented a posterior sampling method for data assimilation that treats stochastic interpolants, flow matching, and denoising diffusion as one family: conditioning the shared interpolant path on an observation turns the marginal-preserving SDE family into a family of provably exact posterior samplers driven by a single likelihood-score term. A closed-form interpolated-observation likelihood supplies that term without inner integrals or trajectory backpropagation, its covariance inflated by the model's own source covariance; the Jacobian-free and ensemble-shared approximations reduce this to one network Jacobian and one $\obsdim\times\obsdim$ factorisation per step, feasible at field scale.

\paragraph{Limitations.}
The exactness guarantees assume a linear observation operator with Gaussian noise, a Gaussian likelihood surrogate whose moments are exact but whose shape is not (except linear--Gaussian), one-step autoregressive assimilation without importance weights or future-observation conditioning, and a perfectly trained drift or velocity; the guided ODE exists only for flow matching. Quantifying the effect of model error on the posterior is left to future work.
